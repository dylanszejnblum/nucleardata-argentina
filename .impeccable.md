## Design Context

### Product
Argentina Nuclear — a bilingual (es default, en) editorial + data publication mapping Argentina's full nuclear stack: uranium deposits, fuel cycle, reactors (Atucha I/II, Embalse, CAREM-25, RA fleet), and medical radioisotope exports (Mo-99, Co-60). Published by **Zirconio**.

### Users
- **Investors / analysts (EN-leaning):** want rigor, data density, project pipeline, ESG context.
- **Industry / policy (ES-leaning):** CNEA/NA-SA/ARN ecosystem, energy sovereignty, SMR roadmap.
- **Curious / editorial readers (both):** want narrative, accessibility, "how does this work."

All three need authority and clarity. Read in both bright offices and dim control-room-like contexts — hence dual equal themes.

### Brand Personality
**Authoritative · Schematic · Quietly optimistic.**

"Authoritative" = data is sourced, lineage is visible, no hype.
"Schematic" = the visual language is engineering drawings, instrument panels, drafting paper.
"Quietly optimistic" = this is atomic-age possibility (Argentina built a complete nuclear stack), not cold tech-bro futurism.

### Emotional Goal
The reader should feel they are reading the definitive, no-nonsense atlas of an industry that almost nobody has visualized end-to-end. Confidence, not spectacle.

### Aesthetic Direction — "Schematic Modernist"
A hybrid of **atomic-age modernism** (1950s engineering posters, geometric optimism, schematic reactor cross-sections as the core visual motif) and **brutalist-science** (heavy display type, exposed grids, monospaced instrument readouts, dense tables that read as authoritative).

Think: **a 1950s IAEA technical manual redesigned by a contemporary Swiss design studio.** Drafting paper in light mode; control-room graticule in dark mode.

### Signature Elements
- **Three.js reactor cutaway** as the site's centerpiece (PHWR / CANDU / CAREM selector). The whole site is "schematic" so this feels native.
- **Dot-grid backgrounds** = graph paper (light) / instrument graticule (dark). Already in the codebase — keep.
- **Monospaced instrument readouts** for all data: KPIs, coordinates, units, timestamps.
- **Uranium-glass green** as the single controlled accent — historically resonant (uranium glass was a 1950s phenomenon), not arbitrary.

### Theme
**Both, equal** — first-class light and dark from day one. User toggle. No default preference beyond respecting `prefers-color-scheme`.
- Light = blueprint / drafting paper / printed manual.
- Dark = control room / instrument panel / graticule.

### Color
- **Accent:** uranium-glass green, OKLCH ~`0.72 0.16 145` (light) / `0.78 0.17 145` (dark). Used sparingly (60-30-10): accents are rare, that's why they register.
- **Neutrals:** tinted toward the green hue (~150°) at very low chroma (0.003–0.01) for subconscious cohesion. Never pure black/white.
- **Functional colors** (success/warning/error) stay muted and distinct from the accent.

### Type System
Sans-display + mono (no serif). All chosen off the reflex-slop list.
- **Display:** `Big Shoulders Display` — ultra-condensed, architectural-drafting inspired. Atomic-poster energy. Headlines + hero numerals only.
- **Body:** `Hanken Grotesk` — clean, slightly humanist grotesque. Highly readable, pairs with the condensed display.
- **Mono:** `Martian Mono` — wide, technical, designed for dense instrument readouts. All data, units, coordinates, eyebrows, labels.

Type rules: fluid clamp scales for marketing headings; tabular-nums on all data; line-height +0.05 on dark backgrounds; cap body at ~70ch.

### Anti-References (explicitly avoid)
- Generic SaaS dashboard with rounded-rectangle-card soup.
- Crypto/DeFi neon-on-dark with glowing accents.
- AI gradient blobs, gradient text, glassmorphism.
- "Inter everywhere" monoculture (Plus Jakarta, Space Grotesk/Mono, IBM Plex also banned — were in this repo).
- Side-stripe accent borders (>1px border-left/right on cards).
- Icons-in-rounded-squares above every heading.

### Design Principles
1. **Schematic over decorative.** When in doubt, draw it like an engineering diagram, not like a marketing site.
2. **Data earns the mono.** Every number, unit, coordinate, timestamp is monospaced and tabular. Prose stays in the grotesque.
3. **Accent is rare.** Uranium green appears only where it carries meaning (live state, the signature, key emphasis). Overuse kills the glow.
4. **Both themes are designed, not inverted.** Light is blueprint, dark is control room — each composed intentionally.
5. **Source everything.** Authority comes from visible data lineage and sourcing, not from visual polish alone.

### Spacing & Layout
4pt scale with semantic tokens. `gap` over margins. Varied rhythm (tight groupings, generous separations). Asymmetric compositions; break the grid for emphasis. Self-adjusting `repeat(auto-fit, minmax())` grids. Cap body text ~70ch.
